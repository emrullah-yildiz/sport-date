01 · the truth
We have never been more connected, and never more alone.

A billion people carry a dating app and still can’t find someone to run with on Saturday. Loneliness is now a measured public-health problem — and the tools built to fix it quietly make it worse.

02 · the insight
Why the tools fail

Connection needs a reason to be in the same place.

Profiles deleted the reason. They turned meeting people into a performance you audition for, and then charged you to keep auditioning. The problem was never a shortage of matches. It’s the absence of a moment.

03 · the uncomfortable incentive
The bug, sold as a feature

Every swipe app profits when you stay. We profit when you leave.

Our incentives point the same way our members do. That alignment is the whole company.

04 · our bet
The solution

Put a real activity first. Let the game carry the encounter.

You join a small local event — a run, a rally, a climb — with the level, time, area and everyone’s intention clear before you request a place. You show up and do something you already enjoy. Connection is a by-product, not a performance.

A good evening remains a good evening, even when romance isn’t the outcome.

05 · what it creates
The outcome we sell
Two people who’d never have swiped right, now training partners.

We don’t measure success in screen time or matches. We measure it in real, completed meetings — and in whether people come back and bring a friend. That’s the number that actually compounds.

North-star: safe, completed encounters per week per neighbourhood — not downloads.

06 · why now
Timing

Two curves are crossing.

Swipe-app trust is falling as run clubs, padel and social-sport communities surge across Europe.

07 · the engine
Why it compounds

Local density is the moat.
