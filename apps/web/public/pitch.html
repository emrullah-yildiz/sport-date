KeepItUp turns a run, a padel match, or a chess game into the way adults meet — for dating, friendship, and community. Europe first.

The problem

Meeting people online got worse, not better.

  • Dating apps ask you to perform through a profile, then audition strangers in a chat that rarely becomes a meeting.
  • Swipe fatigue is real and rising — endless matches, few real connections, growing distrust.
  • Making friends as an adult is genuinely hard; established sports communities are intimidating to enter alone.
  • The result: people are more "connected" and lonelier than ever.
Why now

The shift to activity-led meeting is happening.

  • Swipe-app dissatisfaction is at an all-time high; users are actively leaving for something realer.
  • Run clubs, padel, climbing and social-sport communities are booming across Europe.
  • A loneliness epidemic among 25–39s that governments now treat as a public-health issue.
  • People want fewer, better, in-person connections — not more notifications.
The solution

KeepItUp: the activity carries the first encounter.

Join a small local sports event. The level, time, area and everyone's intention are clear before you request a place. You show up and do something you already enjoy — connection emerges from that, not from a bio.

How it works

Three steps from sign-up to a real meeting.

01

Build a profile

02

Business model

Ethical freemium — safety always free.

  • KeepItUp Plus — €6.99/mo: convenience & richness only (advanced discovery filters, more of what already exists).
  • Free forever: core participation and every safety feature. No paywalled boosts, no paid access to people, no attractiveness/popularity mechanics.
  • Cancel as easily as you subscribe (EU-compliant). Stripe integration built in test mode.
  • Later expansion: host tools, venue partnerships, small event fees — all trust-preserving.
Go-to-market

Organic first, density-led, low CAC.

  • Live now: an agent-run organic social engine (Instagram/TikTok/YouTube) producing on-brand content daily at near-zero cost.
  • Wedge: saturate one European city — reliable hosts, repeat games, real retention — before expanding.
  • Viral surface: shareable event invites + bring-a-friend loops turn events into acquisition.
  • Signal: an in-product research survey collecting willingness-to-pay and demand by city.
