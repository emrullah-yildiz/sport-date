# Experience principles

## Core promise

Sport Date helps adults meet compatible people through small, local sports experiences. The emotional product is not matching; it is the relief of having something real to do together.

## Journey standard

For every feature, consider: discovery, intent, trust check, commitment, coordination, arrival, activity, graceful exit, and reflection. Include rejection, no-show, cancellation, report, block, and emergency paths.

## Design signature

- Use movement as structure: pace, rhythm, anticipation, and recovery.
- Prefer tactile event objects and human language over dashboards and database terminology.
- Show enough humanity to create trust without exposing people to scraping or stalking.
- Make approximate location visibly approximate.
- Let members control whether they seek dating, friendship, or group activity without treating one as a consolation prize.
- Make rejection private and non-punitive. Do not expose skip counts to the requester.
- Avoid infinite feeds, artificial scarcity, manipulative streaks, attractiveness scores, and public popularity metrics.

## Copy test

Copy should sound like a thoughtful host: clear about logistics, calm about safety, and lightly alive. Delete claims that cannot be proved. Replace generic phrases such as “find your perfect match” with concrete moments and outcomes.

## Quality questions

- Would a cautious first-time member understand what happens next?
- Does this screen help a real meeting happen or merely increase engagement?
- What could an abusive member learn, scrape, infer, or exploit here?
- Can someone decline, leave, report, or recover without humiliation?
- Does the mobile experience still work outdoors, one-handed, under time pressure?
