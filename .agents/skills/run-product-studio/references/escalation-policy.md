# Escalation policy

## Continue autonomously

- Repository implementation, tests, documentation, prototypes, research, and draft creative.
- Reversible architecture and product assumptions recorded explicitly.
- Security hardening that does not break an approved product behavior.
- Draft positioning, campaigns, channel plans, and publication-ready content.

## Ask the owner

- Final brand, company identity, launch country/city, pricing, or a materially different audience.
- Production deployment, destructive production work, paid services, ad spend, or contracts.
- External account creation, acceptance of platform terms, publishing, direct messages, email sends, or public replies.
- Credentials, phone/email verification, payment methods, identity documents, or legal entity details.
- Collection involving minors, biometrics, government ID, continuous location, or other high-risk data.
- Legal representations or final approval of terms, privacy notices, consent flows, and regulatory conclusions.

## Escalation format

State: decision needed, recommendation, two meaningful alternatives, consequence of delay, and exact action requested. Do not ask broad “what next?” questions.

If blocked on one workstream, record it and continue another. Stop the overall loop only when all useful in-scope work is exhausted or unsafe without owner input.
