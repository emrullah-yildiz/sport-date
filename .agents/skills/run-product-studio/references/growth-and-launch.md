# Growth and launch

## Positioning discipline

Define one launch audience, one city, one repeated sport occasion, one painful alternative, and one credible reason to believe. Treat “dating plus sports” as a category description, not positioning.

Initial positioning hypothesis: for active adults tired of performative dating profiles, Sport Date turns a first meeting into a shared local activity, so connection can emerge from how people move, cooperate, and show up.

Validate the hypothesis through interviews, waitlist behavior, event-host recruitment, and completed safe events. Do not optimize signups before event liquidity and safety operations are credible.

## Marketplace sequence

1. Choose a dense launch neighborhood and two or three schedule-friendly sports.
2. Recruit reliable founding hosts before acquiring broad member demand.
3. Seed recurring events with clear capacity and cancellation rules.
4. Measure request-to-accept, accept-to-attend, safe completion, repeat attendance, and meaningful connection—not raw swipes.
5. Expand only after the local loop works without heroic manual intervention.

## Social content system

Use four pillars:

- moments: specific scenes that make meeting through sport feel possible;
- usefulness: first-event preparation, safety, etiquette, and hosting;
- belief: the founder's case for changing online dating through real activity;
- building in public: honest product decisions, experiments, and lessons without leaking personal data.

Every post needs a channel, audience tension, single idea, hook, body, CTA, creative brief, accessibility text, and success metric. Adapt rather than cross-posting identical copy.

## External action boundary

Prepare handle research, bios, banners, post drafts, ad concepts, targeting constraints, community replies, and measurement plans autonomously. Request the owner's participation for account creation, identity/phone verification, terms acceptance, credentials, budgets, publishing, replies, partnerships, or platform disputes.
