# Company vision

## The change we want to make

Online dating asks people to perform connection before they have experienced it. Profiles become advertisements, conversations become auditions, and attention becomes the product.

Sport Date begins somewhere more human: two or more people agree to move together. A run supplies rhythm. A tennis game creates play. A climbing session creates small acts of trust. The activity carries the first encounter, leaving people free to notice how someone communicates, encourages, adapts, and shows up.

We are building the social infrastructure for that encounter.

## Vision

Make shared movement the most natural, trusted way for adults to form new relationships in their city.

## Mission

Help adults discover small local sports experiences with compatible people, coordinate safely, and turn online intent into real-world connection.

## What we believe

- Chemistry is easier to discover through a shared experience than through a perfect profile.
- A successful product outcome is a safe, worthwhile encounter—not another hour inside the app.
- Friendship, dating, and community can coexist when intentions are explicit and dignity is protected.
- Trust comes from product behavior and operations, not badges or marketing adjectives.
- Local density and reliable hosts matter more than global download numbers.

## Strategic wedge

Start with adults in one dense European city who already participate in accessible social sports but lack a comfortable way to meet compatible new people. Seed small recurring events through trusted founding hosts. Prove safe attendance and repeat participation before geographic expansion.

The first launch city and sport set remain owner decisions after evidence gathering. Bucharest is the working research hypothesis, not a commitment.

## North-star outcome

Weekly safe completed events where at least two participants say they would willingly meet or play together again.

Supporting measures include request-to-accept rate, acceptance-to-attendance rate, host reliability, report rate and resolution time, repeat participation, and event liquidity by neighborhood and time.

## Non-goals

- Maximizing swipes, screen time, notifications, or artificial urgency.
- Ranking people by attractiveness or public popularity.
- Revealing exact home or event locations during discovery.
- Claiming verification, safety, traction, or relationship outcomes we cannot prove.
