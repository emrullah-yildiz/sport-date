## The spine (read this if nothing else)

1. **TikTok is a cold-start machine; Instagram is a compounding asset.** TikTok's
   algorithm ranks *this video*, not your follower count or history — a brand-new
   account can hit its first "For You" wave on post #1 if watch-through is high.
   Instagram rewards consistency and saves/shares over time and builds a durable
   grid people actually browse before deciding to follow. **Post TikTok to win the
   cold start; post Instagram to convert that attention into a following that
   sticks.**
2. **Shares, saves, and duets/stitches are the currency — not likes.** Every format
   below is scored by what it does for *new* followers and *reach beyond our
   existing audience*, because that's the only thing that gets us from 0 to 1,000.
3. **Five content pillars, rotated weekly, ranked by real data every ~2 weeks.**
   Nothing runs forever out of habit — see `growth-experiments.md` for the
   prune/scale mechanism this strategy feeds.
4. **Ethical trend-jacking is our unfair advantage.** We can't buy reach or fake
   traction, so we ride real cultural moments (sports, seasons, dating-culture
   memes) honestly and fast — see the dedicated method below and the hard rules in
   `trend-jacking-playbook.md`.
5. **The first 100 followers are earned by hand, not by the algorithm.** Seeding
   (below) matters more than posting cadence for weeks 1-3.

## Platform-specific tactics

### Instagram (@keepitup.social)

- **Reels first for reach, carousels for saves.** Reels are IG's non-follower
  discovery surface; carousels are what IG's algorithm weights most for saves
  (people bookmark a "green flags" or "safety checklist" carousel to reread —
  that save is a ranking signal *and* a re-exposure). Run both, track separately.
- **Grid as a landing page.** A cold visitor decides whether to follow by
  skimming the grid for ~3 seconds. Keep the first 9-12 posts a deliberate
  cross-section of all 5 pillars — pin (via first published slot) the single
  carousel that best explains "what is this" (candidate: the "Green Flags You
  Can't Fake" carousel in this batch, or the existing safety carousel).
- **Alt text is search real estate.** IG now surfaces posts in Explore/Search by
  caption + alt text. Every post gets real, descriptive alt text with one or two
  natural keywords ("padel meetup," "activity-led dating," "run club Europe") —
  never keyword-stuffed, always literally true to the image.
- **Native Collabs, not paid shoutouts.** IG's built-in "Collab" post feature lets
  two accounts co-own a single post and both audiences see it in their feed —
  this is organic, ToS-compliant reach-sharing (not a paid placement). Use it with
  amateur-athlete/local-sport micro-creators (see seeding, below).
- **Stories are the low-friction engagement layer.** Daily-ish: polls,
  "this or that," tag-a-friend stickers, and BTS. Stories don't need to be
  polished — they exist to keep the account feeling alive between feed posts and
  to run tag-a-friend/poll mechanics natively.
- **Cadence:** 4 Reels + 2 carousels + 3-4 Stories per week once in steady state
  (see cadence table below for ramp-up).

### TikTok (@keep.it.up988)

- **Hook in the first 1.5 seconds, no logo intro, no slow build.** TikTok's ranking
  doesn't care about account age or follower count on a given video — it cares
  whether people watch past the first second. Cut straight to the pattern
  interrupt.
- **Volume beats polish for the first 60 days.** More videos = more independent
  shots at the "For You" page. Aim for daily posting in weeks 1-8 (see cadence),
  then drop to the format(s) that actually worked.
- **Ride sounds in their first 3-5 days, only when genuinely on-brand.** A trending
  sound loses reach-multiplier value once it's saturated; using it early (and only
  when it fits — never forced) is most of the benefit. See the trend-jacking
  method below.
- **Duet/stitch prompts are a paid-free reach multiplier.** Every duet or stitch of
  our video is shown to *that person's* followers too — "Rate My Date" and similar
  prompts are designed to farm this deliberately.
- **Reply to good comments with a 15-second video reply.** TikTok's comment-reply
  video format surfaces to the original commenter's followers as well — cheap,
  native, and a proven growth lever. Do this weekly on our best-performing post.
- **Native-first, not mirrored.** Only reuse a TikTok concept on IG Reels after it
  proves out, and re-cut it for IG's pacing/captions — never straight re-upload
  (per `social-launch-packet.md`'s "no mirror-upload" rule).

| Find-the-winner | 3-8 | 5 videos/wk, cut anything scoring below-median after ~10 posts | 4 Reels + 2 carousels + 3 Stories/wk | First `growth-experiments.md` review at week 4; prune the bottom format/time-slot. |
| Scale the winner | 9-16 | 4-5 videos/wk, ~70% winning format/pillar, ~20% deliberate variants, ~10% new experiments | 4 Reels + 2 carousels + 3-4 Stories/wk, one Collab/month with a micro-creator | Layer in engagement mechanics (below) at volume; consider first compliant giveaway only once a real prize + T&C page exist. |

Always keep **~20% of any batch as a genuinely new experiment** (new hook style,
new pillar mashup, new time slot) — per the growth-loop mandate, we never stop
discovering the next winner even while scaling a current one.

## The trend-jacking method (condensed — full hard rules in `trend-jacking-playbook.md`)

1. **Scan** daily for genuinely wholesome/playful viral moments: sports feats,
   viral dating-culture memes, seasonal beats (see hooks like `#3` below).
2. **Filter hard** through the playbook's 7 rules — most trends get skipped at
   rule 1 (brand-safe) or rule 4 (genuine tie to "meet through movement"). That's
   correct behavior, not a failure to find content.
3. **Ride the theme, never the person.** Our own branded graphic/script riffing on
   the *vibe* — never a real person's name, photo, likeness, or claimed
   endorsement, never a copyrighted clip/audio.
4. **Move fast but still draft → approve.** Trend windows are days, not weeks; the
   ethics check stays mandatory even under time pressure.
5. **Log it as an experiment.** Every trend-jack post gets a reach/shares
   comparison against our non-trend baseline in `growth-experiments.md` — trend
   posts are how we learn which *themes* (not which trends) work for this
   audience (sports drama vs. dating-culture memes vs. seasonal).

## Engagement mechanics (ethical, ToS-compliant)

All of these run through the standing draft→approve pipeline and stay inside the
hard rules (no coercive gating of the *product*, no fake scarcity, comments/tags
only — never collecting personal data beyond what the platform natively shows).

- **Tag-a-friend.** Cheapest organic reach there is — every tag is a fresh
  impression on someone who doesn't follow us yet. Works best paired with a
  specific, funny relationship in-joke ("tag the friend you'd drag to a padel
  court even though they've never played") rather than a generic "tag a friend."
- **Comment-to-reveal.** A genuine payoff withheld behind a comment prompt — e.g.
  "comment 'RALLY' and I'll reply with the one thing that changed how we run
  these" — *only* when the payoff is real and delivered (curiosity, not bait).
  Doubles as demand-mapping: unique commenters + what they say tells us which
  sports/cities/intents have pull.
- **"Which one are you."** Low-effort-to-engage identity format (three relatable
  archetypes, tag/comment which one). High comment volume, cheap to produce,
  strong "made you laugh, made you share" signal.
- **Duet/stitch/UGC prompts.** "Rate my date," "show us your worst opener" —
  invite the audience to make content *with* us. Each duet is free reach on
  TikTok; feature the best ones (with credit) to reward participation.
- **Comment-to-win (kept from batch 02, still running).** "City + sport" prompts
  drive comments and map real demand; continue only if a real, deliverable,
  honestly-described prize backs any "win" framing — never implied without one.
- **Giveaway (P7, gated).** Do not run until a real prize and a proper T&C/rules
  page exist and the platform's official promotion rules are read — per the
  autopilot runbook's hard boundary. Design is ready in principle; execution
  waits on the owner providing the prize.

## Hashtag & keyword strategy

- **Instagram:** 3-5 hashtags per post, never 20-30 (broad hashtag stuffing is a
  2020s tactic IG's current ranking mostly ignores and can read as spammy). Mix:
  1 branded (`#keepitup`), 1-2 niche-sport (rotate `#padel` `#runclub`
  `#climbing` `#tennis` to match the post's activity), 1-2 category
  (`#datingapps` `#socialsports` `#datingadvice`). Skip mega-generic tags like
  `#love` or `#instagood` — too broad to rank in, no signal value.
- **TikTok:** hashtags matter less than the first 1-3 spoken/on-screen words
  (TikTok's understanding leans on speech-to-text + OCR of on-screen text) — put
  the hook's keyword in the caption's first sentence. Use 3-4 tags: one broad
  (`#datingtok`) only when earned by the content, plus niche (`#padeltok`
  `#runtok`) and category (`#datingadvice` `#datingfatigue`).
- **Anchor phrases (use consistently across both platforms so the algorithms and
  search learn our niche):** "meet through movement," "activity-led dating,"
  "not another dating app," "behavior over bio." Repetition across captions
  (not spam — genuine restatement) trains platform search/recommendation to
  associate us with this specific niche rather than generic "dating app."
- **Alt text (IG) doubles as an SEO surface** — see Instagram tactics above.

## Seeding the first 100 (manual, before the algorithm helps)

The algorithm doesn't work for an account with zero signal. The first ~100
followers are earned by hand:

1. **Genuine participation in niche communities — value first, link second (or
   never).** Local run clubs, padel/tennis Facebook groups, climbing gym
   community boards, Strava clubs, and city-specific subreddits (e.g.
   `r/running`, `r/tennis`, local city subs) often ban drive-by self-promo —
   read each community's rules. Show up as a person with something useful or
   funny to say about activity-led meeting; only mention KeepItUp where the
   community explicitly allows it or someone asks. This is reputational, not
   scalable — budget real founder/agent time for it.
2. **Micro-creator collabs (500-5,000 followers, not mega-influencers).** Find
   amateur athletes and local sport-content creators already making authentic
   run/padel/climbing content. Invite them to one real meetup at no cost, then
   co-create a Reel/TikTok about the actual experience with them tagged/featured
   (native IG Collab where possible). Disclose "partnership" only if anything of
   value beyond attending was exchanged. Their followers are exactly our audience
   and this is organic, not a paid placement.
3. **First-party UGC from real beta testers.** With explicit consent, ask early
   testers to share their own Story/clip about attending — repost with credit.
   Real social proof beats any invented traction claim and satisfies the
   anti-fabrication rule by construction.
4. **Helpful presence in "how do adults meet people" conversations.** Reddit,
   Quora, relevant Facebook groups already discuss dating-app fatigue and
   loneliness — genuinely helpful, non-promotional replies (profile link only
   where allowed) build recognition before any post-based growth kicks in.
5. **Owner's + team's personal network, opt-in only.** Asking friends/family to
   follow if they genuinely want to helps clear the "zero" floor that keeps both
   platforms' algorithms from testing a video/post with anyone. Not coercive,
   not fake — just the normal first-follower bootstrap every new account needs.

Once instagram/tiktok both clear roughly 100 real followers, the algorithmic
tactics above (Reels/TikTok discovery, hashtag/keyword targeting) start doing
more of the work than manual seeding.

## The metric to watch, per format

North star stays **net-new followers**; below is what to look at per format to
explain *why* a post did or didn't produce them (fill into
`growth-experiments.md`'s scorecard):

| Format | Primary metric | Why |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| Reel / TikTok video | Watch-through % + shares | Watch-through is what the algorithm rewards with reach; shares are what turns reach into new followers. |
| Carousel | Saves + last-slide reach (swipe-completion) | Saves are IG's explicit "this is worth keeping" signal; completion tells you the open-loop actually paid off. |
| Meme / single image | Shares + comments | Cheapest, fastest signal — likes are noise here, shares are the whole point. |
| Story | Sticker interactions (tag/poll replies) + profile-visit taps | Stories exist to drive the tap-through to the profile, not standalone reach. |
| Comment-to-reveal / comment-to-win | Unique commenters (not just comment count) | Maps real demand (which city/sport/intent) as much as it drives the ranking signal. |
| Duet/stitch/UGC prompt | Number of duets/stitches + their combined reach | TikTok-specific multiplier metric — a duet's reach is "free" reach we didn't post ourselves. |
| Trend-jack (any format) | Reach/shares lift vs. non-trend baseline of the same format | Confirms whether *this theme* — not just "trends in general" — works for our audience. |

## Milestones, 0 → 1,000

- **0 → 100 (weeks 1-3):** Manual seeding (above) + daily TikTok posting +
  Instagram grid build-out. Win condition: first organically-surfaced TikTok
  video (meaningfully above our average views with no seeding push) or first
  completed micro-creator collab.
- **100 → 400 (weeks 4-8):** First `growth-experiments.md` review at week 4 —
  prune the bottom format and time-slot with real numbers, not guesses. Start
  1 trend-jack/week (opportunistic, never forced) and the first duet/stitch
  prompts.
- **400 → 1,000 (weeks 9-16):** Scale the proven winner at the cadence above,
  layer in tag-a-friend / comment-to-reveal / "which one are you" at volume,
  run 2-3 micro-creator collabs/month. Consider the first compliant giveaway
  only once a real prize and T&C page exist (owner-gated).

Review cadence throughout: **every ~2 weeks**, per `growth-experiments.md` —
measure, prune, double down, keep ~20% new bets.
