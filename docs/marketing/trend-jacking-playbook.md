# Trend-jacking playbook — riding trends, ethically

Owner directive (2026-07-04): tie content to trending moments (e.g. "the goalkeeper
everyone's talking about", "the couple who climbed the tower") — but **ethical posts only.**
Newsjacking is high-reach and high-risk. These rules are non-negotiable.

## The 7 hard rules (never break)

1. **Brand-safe trends only.** Tie to positive/playful/relevant moments: viral sports
   feats, wholesome or funny viral moments, seasonal/cultural beats, big sporting events.
   **NEVER** tragedy, death, disaster, crime victims, illness, politics, war, protests,
   scandal, or anything where people are hurting. If in doubt, skip it.
2. **No real person's likeness, name, photo, or a brand's logo without rights.** We make
   **our own branded graphics riffing on the THEME**, not the person. No screenshots, no
   copyrighted images/clips/audio, no deepfakes, no implying a real person endorses us.
3. **Be factually honest.** Don't invent or misstate what happened. Reference the *vibe*
   or *theme*, not false specifics. If we can't verify a claim, we don't assert it.
4. **The tie must be genuine.** It has to connect naturally to "meet through movement"
   (sport, activity, adventure, connection). No forced/random hijacking for clout.
5. **Punch up, never down.** Kind, inclusive, self-aware. Never mock a person, a group,
   an identity, a body, or someone's misfortune. We're the warm one.
6. **Timely, but ethics-reviewed.** Trends are time-sensitive — move fast, but every
   trend post still passes the ethics check + draft→approve before it publishes.
7. **Respect platform ToS + law.** No misinformation, no misuse of trademarks, no
   defamation, no using someone's image commercially without consent.

## The process (repeatable)

1. **Scan** current trends daily (what's genuinely viral this week — sports moments,
   wholesome viral clips, seasonal beats). Verify the facts before using them.
2. **Filter** through the 7 rules. Most trends fail rule 1 or 4 — that's fine, skip them.
3. **Find the on-brand angle** — how does this connect to meeting through movement?
4. **Produce a branded graphic** in the KeepItUp system (our template, our voice — the
   THEME, not the real people). Reuse the meme/quote-card templates.
5. **Caption** — witty, warm, ties to our value prop, honest.
6. **Draft → approve → publish** inside the trend's window (fast, but reviewed).
7. **Measure** — trend posts are experiments; log reach/shares in `growth-experiments.md`.

## Good vs bad (examples)

- ✅ *Viral daring "skyscraper date" is everywhere* → "You don't need to scale a
  skyscraper for a memorable first date — a climbing wall works." (theme, not the people;
  ties to our climbing activity; playful).
- ✅ *A goalkeeper's heroic save goes viral* → "Some people find their perfect match at a
  match." (evergreen sports-romance angle; no real name/photo).
- ✅ *Marathon season / a big city run trends* → seasonal run-club tie-in.
- ❌ Using the actual person's photo/name, or claiming they use KeepItUp.
- ❌ Joking about anyone's arrest, injury, breakup, or a tragedy for reach.
- ❌ Forcing an unrelated celebrity scandal into our feed.

## Governance

Trend posts run through the social-media-growth agent under the standing draft→approve
model. Because they're time-sensitive AND higher-risk, the ethics check (the 7 rules) is
mandatory and logged; anything borderline is escalated to the owner, not shipped.
