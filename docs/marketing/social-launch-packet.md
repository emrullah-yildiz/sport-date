# Social launch packet

This is a preparation document. No account may be created or content published without the owner's explicit authorization, platform-term acceptance, and credentials.

The evidence-backed Bucharest research copy, bilingual host outreach, form fields, campaign tags, and measurement rules live in [bucharest-research-campaign.md](./bucharest-research-campaign.md). Use that packet for research recruitment; keep the broader concepts below for a later product launch.

## Recommended initial accounts

- Instagram: visual proof of the feeling, host stories, event etiquette, and founder belief.
- TikTok: short observations about profile-first dating and activity-led connection; use only if repeatable native video production is realistic.
- LinkedIn: founder narrative, trust-and-safety decisions, partnerships, hiring, and building in public.
- YouTube Shorts can reuse concepts only after channel-specific editing; do not mirror-upload automatically.

Reserve matching handles on major platforms once the final brand and trademark screen are approved. Prepare a recovery email, password manager, two-factor authentication, named owner, backup administrator, and access log before account creation.

## Working profile copy

Name: Sport Date — working name

Bio: Meet through movement. Small local sports experiences for adults who want dating, friendship, or community without the endless swiping. Open worldwide — early access.

Do not state a launch city until selected.

## Content pillars

1. The moment: recognizable scenes where sport makes meeting easier.
2. The playbook: useful hosting, attendance, etiquette, and safety guidance.
3. The belief: the founder's argument for real experiences over attention loops.
4. Building honestly: decisions, prototypes, tests, and lessons without fabricated traction.

## First six post briefs

1. “A first date where silence is allowed.” Show the natural pauses and shared focus of a rally or run. CTA: join the research list.
2. “Your profile cannot show how you encourage someone.” Founder-led argument for behavior as compatibility. CTA: comment with the sport that reveals character.
3. “What you should know before joining a stranger's game.” Practical safety carousel; only mention implemented controls.
4. “Dating, friendship, or just the game?” Explain explicit intentions without hierarchy or shame.
5. “We hide the exact meeting point on purpose.” Build-in-public privacy decision and threat model.
6. “Could three people and a tennis court change how a city meets?” Vision post focused on small local density rather than downloads.

Each post still needs channel-specific copy, creative, alt text, owner approval, and a measurement tag before publishing.

## Community response rules

Never diagnose, argue with safety reports, expose personal data, promise outcomes, or move sensitive complaints into casual direct messages. Acknowledge concerns, preserve evidence, and route them into the formal moderation process once it exists.
