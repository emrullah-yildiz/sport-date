## New standing rule: branded poster look

**Owner directive (2026-07-05, added to this agent's brief):** every image must look like a
company poster — the KeepItUp logo/mark visible, branded text blocks in the brand type system,
on the anthracite (`#20262B`) / neon-green (`#3BEA7E`) / off-white (`#F1F5F3`) palette — not a
bare stock photo with a caption underneath. This is a real change from batches 06-08, where
several posts used a **clean-photo, caption-led** treatment with no logo on the image at all.
Every post in this batch (images and carousels alike) now carries:

- **KeepItUp mark** (`keepitup-mark-transparent-1024.png`), small, one corner, every slide.
- **Eyebrow/headline block** in the brand type system, off-white on an anthracite scrim panel
  (for legibility over a photo), neon-green for the eyebrow label or a key word.
- **Footer band**: thin neon-green rule + `keepitup-wordmark-transparent.png` + a small
  "keepitup.social · open worldwide, early access" imprint line — the way a real company signs
  a poster.
- Position/exact overlay text is specified per post below. `assets` in the JSON companion point
  at the **planned composite path** (`/brand/social/photos/b9pNN-*.png`); the base photo is named
  in each post's `imageConcept` — composites are baked before Buffer seeding, per the existing
  batch 08 pattern.

**New hypothesis added to `growth-experiments.md`:** `H-BRAND-posterlook` — does visible logo +
branded poster treatment lift saves and profile-visit clicks vs. our prior treatments (clean-photo
caption-led, or overlay-with-scrim but no visible logo)? Four posts below (P1-P4) are direct,
same-joke/same-pillar A/B pairs against an already-shipped prior version, specifically to test
this in isolation.
