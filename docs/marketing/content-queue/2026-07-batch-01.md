## 03 — "What to know before you join a stranger's game"
- Status: `draft`
- Primary: Instagram carousel
- Pillar: The playbook (safety) — mention ONLY implemented controls

**Caption:**
Meeting someone new should feel exciting, not risky. A few things we do (and that you
should always do):

2) You decide what you share and when — exact meeting points aren't shown during discovery.

## 05 — "We hide the exact meeting point on purpose"
- Status: `draft`
- Primary: X + LinkedIn (build-in-public)
- Pillar: Building honestly

**Caption:**
A design decision we're proud of: during discovery, KeepItUp shows an approximate area —
never the exact meeting point or anyone's home.

Why: a stranger browsing events should never be able to pin down where a real person will
physically be. The precise spot appears only when it's appropriate, to people who are
actually part of the plan.

Privacy isn't a setting we bolted on. It's a default we designed around. More of these
decisions to come as we build in the open.

**Hashtags:** #buildinpublic #privacybydesign #trustandsafety #sportdate #startup
**Alt text:** Map showing a soft approximate circle over a neighborhood, not a pin.
**Visual direction:** Stylized map with a fuzzy area (not a pin), brand colors. On LinkedIn, expand into a short founder note.
