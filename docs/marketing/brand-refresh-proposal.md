# Brand refresh proposal — name & logo exploration

Status: **owner-decision draft.** This is a design/marketing exploration, not a decision and
not a legal opinion. Per `references/escalation-policy.md`, final brand and company identity are
**owner decisions**. Nothing in the app has been renamed or re-logo'd. The owner picks; agents may
build only the *swap mechanism* (see `CX-20260701-brand-asset-swap-mechanism.md`).

The trademark / SEO notes below are **flags for the owner to investigate with counsel and a proper
search**, not clearances or conclusions.

---

## 1. Keep or rename "Sport Date"?

### What the current name does well

- **Instantly legible category.** "Sport" + "Date" tells a first-time visitor, in two words, that
  this is about meeting people *through sport*. No tagline required to grasp the premise.
- **On-strategy with the vision.** The vision (`docs/company/vision.md`) and positioning
  (`docs/marketing/positioning.md`, brand promise "Meet through movement") are literally *movement
  as the first encounter*. "Sport Date" encodes that in the name.
- **Pronounceable and typeable** in both English and Romanian (the working research market). Short,
  no diacritics, no ambiguous spelling.
- **Domain/handle friendly** as a compound (easy to build a wordmark around two clean syllable
  blocks).

### The risks (why the owner asked to reconsider)

- **"Date" skews purely romantic.** The vision is explicit that *friendship, dating, and community
  can coexist* and that "framing friendship as failure" is a non-goal. "Date" in the name can signal
  *dating-app*, which:
  - narrows perceived audience (people who want a running partner or a group, not a romance, may
    self-exclude);
  - inherits swipe-app baggage the product is deliberately *not* (profiles-as-ads, attention-as-
    product);
  - can raise a safety/expectation mismatch — someone arriving expecting a "date" vs. a host arriving
    to run.
- **"Date" is overloaded.** Calendar date vs. romantic date. "Sport date" as a phrase already means
  "the date a match is played" in sports-scheduling contexts, which muddies search intent.
- **Generic, hard-to-own words.** "Sport" and "Date" are both high-frequency dictionary words.
  - **Trademark-collision caution (FLAG, not a conclusion):** highly descriptive marks are harder to
    protect and more likely to collide with existing "sport" / "date" marks across the EU and
    globally. The owner should run a proper EUIPO/national + common-law search with counsel before
    committing.
  - **SEO caution (FLAG):** "sport date" as a query is dominated by sports-fixture/schedule results;
    ranking a brand for it organically would be an uphill, expensive fight.
- **Not distinctive.** Little brand memorability or defensibility; hard to build a durable, ownable
  identity around two generic words.

### Recommendation (owner's call)

**Explore a rename toward a distinctive, movement-first name that keeps warmth and does not lock the
product into "dating" only** — but do not rush it. "Sport Date" is a *serviceable* working name and
is fine to keep operating under during research. The strongest move is to (a) shortlist candidates
below, (b) run trademark/domain/handle checks, (c) test 2-3 with the member survey (see
`docs/marketing/member-survey-and-forum-kit.md`), and (d) decide once. Recommendation leans **rename**
primarily to shed the romance-only read and gain distinctiveness/defensibility.

This recommendation is captured for decision in `CX-20260701-owner-decision-brand-name-and-logo.md`
(`blocked-owner`).

## 2. Name candidates

Each is a *direction to test*, not a cleared mark. All need trademark/domain/handle checks by the
owner before use. Scored informally on: distinctiveness, movement-first fit, inclusivity of
friendship+group+dating, and ease of pronunciation/spelling.

| Candidate | One-line rationale | Vision fit |
|---|---|---|
| **Meetmove** / **MeetMove** | Literally "meet + move" — the whole thesis in one coined compound; ownable, not romance-locked. | Direct restatement of brand promise "Meet through movement." Includes friendship/group/dating equally. |
| **Movemate** | Warm, human ("mate" = companion, not romance); "move" keeps the movement wedge. | Signals companionship of any kind; friendly, low-pressure, non-audition. |
| **Rally** | A rally is both a tennis exchange *and* people coming together; short, energetic, memorable. | Movement + gathering in one word; sport-native; no romance skew. (High collision risk — many "Rally" marks exist; treat as a strong-vibe candidate that likely needs a modifier, e.g. "Rally Sport".) |
| **Sidekick** | The person you show up with; playful, warm, dignity-preserving, zero swipe baggage. | Companionship-first; fits "someone to do something real with"; inclusive of all intentions. |
| **Tempo** | Shared pace/rhythm — the design signature is literally "movement as structure: pace, rhythm." | Encodes the product's own design language; calm, adult, not gimmicky. |
| **Warmup** / **Warm Up** | The low-pressure beginning of movement *and* of a relationship; disarming, honest about "no pressure." | Matches "relief of having something real to do together"; anti-audition; friendly. |

Notes for the owner's shortlist:
- **Meetmove** and **Movemate** are the safest on *distinctiveness + inclusivity + on-strategy*.
- **Rally** and **Tempo** are the most evocative but likely the most crowded trademark-wise.
- **Warmup** and **Sidekick** best defuse the romance-only read and feel warmest.
- Avoid any candidate that re-introduces "date"/"match"/"swipe" — those re-import the exact baggage
  the rename is meant to shed, and "match" collides with the incumbent category leader.

## 3. Logo / wordmark directions

Three directions described so the owner can react. All use the design-system palette
(`docs/design-system.md`): Ink `#17241d`, Cream `#f4f0e7`, Lime `#c9f458`, Coral `#ff765f`,
Sage `#667169`. All must meet the system rule: visible focus, readable at small sizes, works
in light/dark, reduced-motion safe (a static logo is inherently reduced-motion safe; any animated
variant must have a static fallback).

### Direction A — "Motion mark" (recommended)

A compact geometric glyph suggesting *movement/momentum* (a forward-leaning arc, running stride, or
rally arc) in Lime, paired with a bold compact wordmark in Ink on Cream. The glyph doubles as the app
icon/favicon at 32px. Energetic, warm, sport-native without a literal ball/racket (which would lock
to one sport).

### Direction B — "Two dots meeting"

Two rounded marks (one Ink, one Lime) converging along a shared path — literally *two people meeting
through movement*. Reads as connection + motion; scales down cleanly; naturally inclusive of pairs
*and* groups (add a third dot for the group story). Risk: "two dots" is a common device — needs a
distinctive execution to be ownable.

### Direction C — "Wordmark-only, rhythm-spaced"

No glyph; a confident lowercase wordmark where letter-spacing/weight creates a sense of *pace* (e.g.
a subtle baseline lift or a single Lime accent letter). Cheapest to produce and license, most
flexible; leans on typography (the system already favors "bold, compact headlines"). Best if the
chosen *name* is itself distinctive enough to carry the brand.

### Buildable inline SVG wordmark concept (Direction C, placeholder name "Meetmove")

This is a **concept**, on-palette, buildable today, and deliberately name-agnostic so the owner can
drop in the chosen word. It renders as a wordmark with a single Lime accent and a small motion arc.
It is static (reduced-motion safe by construction) and legible at small sizes.

```svg
        stroke="#c9f458" stroke-width="4" stroke-linecap="round"/>
  <!-- wordmark: bold, compact, Ink -->
  <text x="60" y="34" font-family="'Segoe UI', system-ui, sans-serif"
        font-size="28" font-weight="800" letter-spacing="-0.5" fill="#17241d">meet<tspan fill="#667169">move</tspan></text>
  <!-- accent dot: 'the meeting', Lime -->
  <circle cx="212" cy="16" r="4" fill="#c9f458"/>
</svg>
```

Swap the `<text>`/`aria-label` for the chosen name and re-tune the arc endpoint to the wordmark width.
Provide a Cream-on-Ink inverse variant for dark surfaces, and a glyph-only 32px favicon crop of the
arc + dot for the app icon.

## 4. What happens next (owner)

1. Owner reviews §1-§3 and either **keeps "Sport Date"** or **picks a rename direction** (name +
   logo direction), recorded on `CX-20260701-owner-decision-brand-name-and-logo.md`.
2. Owner (or counsel) runs **trademark + domain + handle + SEO** checks on the shortlist — the flags
   above are not clearances.
3. Independently of the decision, engineering can build the **brand asset swap mechanism**
   (`CX-20260701-brand-asset-swap-mechanism.md`, `ready`, no rename performed) so that whatever the
   owner picks changes in one place across the whole app.
4. Optionally, 2-3 finalist names are tested in the member survey
   (`docs/marketing/member-survey-and-forum-kit.md`) before the final lock.
