# Bucharest discovery interview kit

Use this to learn from behavior, not to sell the concept. Ask permission before taking notes. Do not record or collect special-category data unless a reviewed consent and retention process exists.

## Recruiting targets

- 20 prospective members matching the working audience hypothesis.
- 8 prospective hosts: four running/run-walk, two padel, two bouldering reserves.
- Seek variation across gender, relationship intent, Romanian/English preference, experience, schedule, neighborhood, travel mode, and accessibility needs.
- Do not recruit only from the founder's friends or one existing club.

Offer the same modest incentive to every participant if approved. Keep contact details separate from notes, assign an interview ID, and publish no quote or identity without specific permission.

## Member script (30 minutes)

Opening: “We are researching how adults in Bucharest meet through sport. This is not a sales call, there is no live service to join today, and you can skip any question. May I take written notes for product research?”

1. Tell me about the last time you wanted to meet someone new in Bucharest.
2. What did you actually do? Walk me through it from the first thought to the outcome.
3. When did a dating app, sports group, friend introduction, or event last work well for you? What made it work?
4. When did one fail or feel uncomfortable? What happened?
5. Which sports or movement activities have you done in the past 60 days? With whom, where, and how often?
6. How do you decide whether to join a group with people you do not know?
7. What information must you know before committing? What should stay private until later?
8. What makes a host credible? What would make you leave or report an event?
9. How far and how long did you travel for your last social activity?
10. Think about your next two weeks. Which evenings or weekend windows are realistically available?
11. Show two concrete cards: a free Tuesday run-walk near Aviatorilor and a four-person indoor padel game near Aurel Vlaicu with the actual expected price. Ask: “What would you do next?” Do not ask whether they “like” the idea.
12. What would stop you from requesting a place in each one?
13. Would you want dating, friendship, or either represented? What would make mixed intentions respectful or awkward?
14. Who else should we learn from, especially someone who would disagree with you?

Do not lead with features, ask participants to predict mass-market demand, or accept “sounds cool” as evidence.

## Host script (40 minutes)

1. Tell me about the last event or group session you organized.
2. How did people find it, commit, pay, cancel, and show up?
3. Where does coordination consume the most effort?
4. Describe the last mismatch in level, expectations, or behavior. What did you do?
5. What is the practical minimum and maximum group size for a good session?
6. Which costs, deposits, venue terms, waivers, equipment, and weather fallbacks matter?
7. What information do you need about a participant? What should you not receive?
8. When would you decline someone? How should that decision be communicated?
9. Describe a safety concern you have handled or would not feel qualified to handle.
10. Could you host the same bounded format four times in one month? Ask for actual dates, not general interest.
11. What support, compensation, training, or partnership terms would be necessary?
12. What would make you refuse to associate your name or community with this product?

Never imply that a host is a moderator, emergency service, employee, or “verified safe” unless the actual legal and operational relationship supports it.

## Observation and scoring

After the call, separately score 0-2 for each item:

| Signal | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Problem evidence | hypothetical | old/vague example | recent specific behavior |
| Current workaround | none | occasional | repeated time/money cost |
| Event commitment | praise only | conditional interest | chooses real time/price |
| Travel fit | outside tolerance | uncertain | corridor fits routine |
| Safety fit | unresolved blockers | answerable concern | controls match need |
| Host reliability (hosts) | no history | irregular | repeated evidence + dates |

Record the strongest direct behavior, biggest objection, and assumption changed. Quotes stay internal unless separately authorized for publication.

## Decision review

Review after 10 member interviews, after 20, and after all 8 host interviews. Count behavior-backed commitments separately from compliments and report disconfirming evidence. The owner approves real outreach, incentives, partnerships, and public recruitment copy before use.
