# Bucharest launch wedge research

Evidence reviewed on 2026-06-29. This recommends the next validation test, not the owner's final launch decision.

## Recommendation

Test a **north Bucharest corridor from Aviatorilor / Herastrau to Aurel Vlaicu / Floreasca** with two formats:

1. **Beginner-friendly social run or run-walk**: 6-10 people, 45 minutes, one fixed weekday evening, public-park start area, no pace pressure.
2. **Beginner-friendly padel**: exactly 4 people, 60-90 minutes, one fixed weekday evening or weekend slot, indoor court, equipment available.

Use running to test recurring local demand cheaply. Use padel to test the strongest small-group social shape: a bounded booking, natural rotation, four seats, and conversation between points. Do not launch a third sport until these produce host and member evidence. Keep bouldering as the first reserve because it is year-round and conversation-friendly, but its less bounded group structure fits the current capacity model less cleanly.

This is a corridor experiment, not a promise to serve all of Bucharest. Discovery should initially show only events in this corridor and never expose exact meeting points before acceptance.

## Why this corridor

- 321sport advertises a recurring Tuesday 19:30 run in Parcul Regele Mihai I, showing that a predictable after-work running habit already exists. This proves category behavior, not demand for Sport Date.
- Padel One describes 11 courts, including 8 indoor courts, near Aurel Vlaicu metro in Floreasca. Indoor supply reduces weather risk and shares the M2 corridor with Aviatorilor.
- Metrorex's network map places Aviatorilor and Aurel Vlaicu consecutively on M2. Its accessibility list records lifts and other access equipment at both stations. This does not prove door-to-door convenience; interviews must test actual travel tolerance.
- The corridor has credible recurring sport supply, but likely has higher prices and may over-represent affluent north-city residents. Recruitment must test whether the wedge feels exclusive, inconvenient, or unaffordable.

## Local evidence

### Running

- [321sport](https://www.321sport.ro/) publishes recurring Tuesday 19:30 runs in Parcul Regele Mihai I and Thursday sessions at Lia Manoliu. Its historical program pages say thousands have joined over multiple years.
- [ARITA](https://arita.run/) presents a Bucharest run-walk community with weekly, no-pressure, all-level formats and free access. Verify its 2026 claims directly because the site also says parts of its club/payment operation are pending registration.

Interpretation: repeated social running is already legible in Bucharest. Sport Date must not compete as another generic run club; its test is whether explicit relationship intentions, small groups, compatibility, and protected coordination solve a separate job.

### Padel

- [Padel One](https://www.padelone.ro/despre-padel-one-club) describes a Floreasca club near Aurel Vlaicu metro with 11 courts, 8 indoor and 3 outdoor, and positions the sport as social and accessible.
- [Padel World](https://www.padelworld.ro/) lists Bucharest-area indoor courts and public prices from 140-190 RON per court-hour at its Royal location, plus racket rental. Recheck prices and availability before offering an event.
- [Magic Smash](https://magicsmash.ro/) advertises five covered courts at Sema Parc for beginner through advanced players, indicating supply beyond the proposed corridor.

Interpretation: visible venue supply justifies player and partner interviews. It does not establish occupancy, member demand, partnership interest, or safe-event economics.

### Bouldering reserve

- [Blokx](https://blokx.ro/) advertises all-level bouldering, weekly new routes, courses, a cafe/bar, and events at Fabrica de Glucoza in Sector 2.

Interpretation: bouldering has strong conversation and year-round properties. Keep it as the replacement if padel cost or booking friction fails, rather than fragmenting the first marketplace across three sports.

### Access and city context

- [Metrorex's official network map](https://www.metrorex.ro/storage/documents/1746817266HartaMtx%202024%20en.pdf) shows the M2 relationship among central Bucharest, Aviatorilor, and Aurel Vlaicu.
- [Metrorex's accessibility inventory](https://metrorex.ro/statii_metrou_accesibile) lists access equipment by station.
- The [Bucharest Green City Action Plan](https://paov.pmb.ro/docs/DLV_3_PUF_Report_V3_EN_clean.pdf), published through the municipality, describes Bucharest as concentrating about 9.8% of Romania's officially resident population while noting infrastructure and environmental constraints. Treat planning figures as context, not proof of addressable dating demand.

## Audience hypothesis

Start interviews with single adults aged 25-39 who live or work within a practical M2 trip of the corridor, can communicate in Romanian or English, already do a social sport at least twice per month, and are open to dating while accepting that a worthwhile friendship or game is not failure.

Do not exclude people from the product based on income, athletic appearance, disability, ethnicity, nationality, or sexual orientation. The narrow interview frame is a liquidity experiment. Relationship modes and inclusion requirements need explicit research before public launch.

## Founding-host criteria

A founding host must:

- already organize or reliably attend the chosen sport;
- welcome beginners without pretending to coach beyond their competence;
- run a repeatable format with a clear start, end, capacity, level, price, and cancellation rule;
- agree not to reveal exact locations outside accepted-member access;
- understand that host status is not safety certification;
- follow the code of conduct, escalation path, incident preservation, and no-retaliation rule;
- commit to at least four pilot sessions and a short operational debrief after each;
- avoid alcohol-led formats during the initial pilot;
- never use access to recruit privately, pressure romantic attention, or bypass blocking.

Recruit **four hosts before member acquisition**: two run/run-walk hosts and two padel hosts, with at least one backup per format. No paid acquisition begins until the team can publish four weeks of credible event supply.

## Validation plan and kill criteria

### Phase 1: 20 interviews and 8 host conversations

Continue only if:

- at least 12 of 20 target members describe a recent, concrete problem meeting compatible people through current dating or sport options;
- at least 8 would consider one proposed format in the next 30 days at a stated time and real price;
- at least 4 host candidates meet the criteria and will commit to four sessions;
- safety concerns can be answered with implemented controls and staffed operations, not marketing language.

### Phase 2: concierge waitlist test

Create no fake profiles, testimonials, counters, or events. Continue only if 40 qualified people join from direct/community recruitment and at least 20 select a real proposed time. Track corridor, sport, intent, language, level, price tolerance, and accessibility needs with explicit consent and data minimization.

### Phase 3: closed pilot

Run 8-12 hosted events without paid ads. Continue only if:

- at least 70% of accepted places become self-confirmed attendance;
- at least 60% of attendees say they would willingly meet or play with someone from the event again;
- at least 3 of 4 hosts choose to host again;
- critical reports receive the staffed response required by the safety plan;
- no repeated pattern shows that price, travel, exclusion, host behavior, or exact-location handling makes the format unacceptable.

Pause or replace the wedge if host supply cannot be secured, padel economics exclude the audience, repeated events cannot fill without broad city-wide travel, or safety staffing is not live. Replace padel with bouldering before adding more neighborhoods or sports.

## Unknowns before outreach

- Final relationship modes, sexual-orientation inclusion, and how mixed intentions appear in a small event.
- Whether 25-39 is the right liquidity band or an unnecessarily broad/young frame.
- Real door-to-door travel tolerance and whether north Bucharest feels exclusionary.
- Venue cancellation, payment, waiver, equipment, photography, and liability terms.
- Host compensation and whether payment changes employment, consumer, tax, or platform obligations.
- Accessibility of specific routes and venues, not merely metro stations.
- Qualified Romanian/EU counsel review and a named safety rota before live member messaging.
