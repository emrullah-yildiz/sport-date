# Member jobs and journeys

## Beachhead hypotheses

### The active newcomer

Recently moved, changed routines, or lost a regular sports group. Wants familiar movement and low-pressure human contact without entering an established club alone.

Job: “Help me find a small activity I can confidently show up to, with people likely to welcome my level and intentions.”

### The intentional dater

Tired of profile-first dating and repetitive chats. Wants to observe compatibility through behavior while keeping the meeting structured and public.

Job: “Help me meet someone in a way that feels real, safe, and worthwhile even if there is no romantic chemistry.”

### The community host

Already initiates games or knows a venue. Wants reliable participants, clear expectations, and tools to handle approvals, cancellations, and bad behavior.

Job: “Help me fill the right places with compatible people without turning coordination into a second job.”

## End-to-end journey

1. Understand the promise and what Sport Date is not.
2. Create a private adult profile with intentions, languages, sports, and experience.
3. Discover an event using approximate location, time, level, language, capacity, and host signals.
4. Evaluate trust: logistics, expectations, cancellation policy, safety controls, and what becomes visible when.
5. Request a place with a short human introduction.
6. Receive a dignified acceptance, decline, expiry, or wait state.
7. For accepted participants, enter the event room and receive precise details at the appropriate time.
8. Coordinate without sharing private contact information.
9. Arrive, check in, participate, leave, or get help.
10. Provide private safety feedback and choose whether to reconnect, repeat, block, or report.

## Required edge journeys

- Host cancels, venue changes, weather disruption, late arrival, no-show, and event capacity changes.
- Request remains unanswered, is skipped three times, or is declined.
- Participant feels unsafe before arrival, during the event, or afterward.
- Someone is blocked while sharing an event room.
- Report involves the host, multiple participants, or offline conduct.
- Member requests account export or deletion.

These are product journeys, not “support later” work.
